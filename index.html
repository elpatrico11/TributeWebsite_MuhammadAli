Muhammad Ali

A boxer who moved like a dancer

Nicknamed "the Greatest", he is regarded as one of the most significant sports figures of the 20th century and is often regarded as the greatest heavyweight boxer of all time.


Here's a time line of Muhammad Ali's life:

  • 1942 - Muhammad Ali is born on January 17th in Louisville, Kentucky
  • 1954 - Ali begins boxing at the age of 12 after his bicycle is stolen and he vows to "whup" the thief
  • 1960 - Wins a gold medal in the light heavyweight division at the Rome Olympics
  • 1964 - Becomes the heavyweight champion of the world by defeating Sonny Liston on February 25th and joins the Nation of Islam, changing his name to Muhammad Ali
  • 1967 - Refuses to be inducted into the U.S. Army, citing religious reasons and his opposition to the Vietnam War, leading to his boxing license being suspended and his title stripped
  • 1970 - Returns to boxing after a three-year exile.
  • 1974 - Wins back the heavyweight title by defeating George Foreman in the famous "Rumble in the Jungle" in Kinshasa, Zaire.
  • 1975 - Defeats Joe Frazier in the "Thrilla in Manila," a brutal and legendary fight
  • 1978 - Loses and then regains the heavyweight title in fights against Leon Spinks
  • 1980 - Retires from boxing with a professional record of 56 wins and 5 losses
  • 1996 - Lights the Olympic flame at the Summer Olympics in Atlanta
  • 2016 - Dies on June 3rd in Scottsdale, Arizona, after a long battle with Parkinson's disease

"Only a man who knows what it is like to be defeated can reach down to the bottom of his soul and come up with the extra ounce of power it takes to win when the match is even."

-- Muhammad Ali
